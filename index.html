Research Interests

Computational Neuroscience Neuromorphic Computing Neural Modeling Machine Learning for Biomedical Systems Brain-Inspired AI Brain-Computer Interface Neural Networks

My research journey is driven by a fundamental question: How can we bridge the gap between biological intelligence and artificial systems? The brain, with its remarkable efficiency and adaptability, offers a blueprint for the next generation of computing architectures.

I am particularly fascinated by the computational principles underlying neural information processing. Through my work in spiking neuron simulations and biophysical modeling, I aim to understand not just what neurons do, but how they do it‚Äîand how we can translate these mechanisms into practical neuromorphic systems that could revolutionize fields from drug discovery to brain-computer interfaces.

My interdisciplinary background in mathematics and ongoing studies in AI equip me to approach these challenges from both theoretical and applied perspectives. I believe the future of artificial intelligence lies in embracing the biological principles that have evolved over millions of years.

Research Vision

My research interests focus on computational neuroscience and neuromorphic computing, with an emphasis on brain-inspired models for efficient intelligence. I am interested in understanding how biological principles such as spike-based communication, synaptic plasticity, and neural dynamics can be translated into spiking neural networks and low-power learning systems. Through research internships and project-based work, I have developed experience in neural modeling, machine learning, and signal analysis. My long-term goal is to pursue doctoral research that integrates theoretical modeling with practical neuromorphic and AI applications.
